INSTRUCTIONS TO USE

• This interactive guide will support your learning and practice on referencing your sources and including them in your academic written work.
• You can navigate this guide according to your needs, remember you can come back any time you feel you need to.
• You can get the downloaded file and use it offline.
• There is a speaker icon at the top left, you can use the narrated version of this guide if you prefer.
• There will be interactive activities for each kind of source to reference, do not worry if you don't get it right at the first attempt, there is no attempts limit
• You can click on the 'Question Mark' button to get a hint on what you are missing or forgetting in your answer.
• The 'Learn More' section will provide you with a variety of external links to videos, web pages, comparative charts and referencing manager tools that you can access if you want to deepen your learning
• In the 'Learn More' section you can also get the link where you can book an appointment with the librarian for a 30-minutes tutorial.
• The 'Ask&Contribute' section will take you to a Padlet board where you can ask any question that has been unsolved in the guide, ask for support if you have a source that you find hard to reference, you can also answer someone's questions or share additional tools and links that you have tried and think that may be useful for other students.
• Please feel free to send us your feedback after you have used this referencing guide, it would be very helpful to hear your opinions and suggestions to provide a better experience.
